## Copy Inputs

Before drafting a page, identify:

- Target buyer or buying group.
- Travel occasion.
- Package and factual promise.
- Current pressure, frustration, or missed experience.
- Desired emotional and practical outcome.
- Main trust barrier.
- Main price or effort objection.
- Proof available on the page.
- Smallest useful next step.

If the page is broad, write to the shared local desire for a clear, trustworthy, well-planned Kenyan escape. If the page receives paid campaign traffic, choose one dominant audience and angle.

## Value Equation Adaptation

`Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort and Sacrifice)`

### Increase the dream outcome

- Describe how the traveler wants to feel, not only what the itinerary contains.
- Connect the trip to rest, pride, romance, family memory, friendship, achievement, or organizer competence.
- Make the destination tangible through specific experiences.

### Increase perceived likelihood

- Name the duration, route, vehicle, accommodation basis, meals, activities, guide, park fees, and exclusions.
- Show approved photographs of what the buyer will actually experience.
- Explain that Holiday Kenya Safaris is operated by Ashford Tours & Travel.
- Use real Kenyan reviews and operational proof only when supplied or verified.
- Make the quote process and human follow-up clear.

### Reduce time delay

- Show the itinerary immediately.
- Give a visible provisional `From KSh...` price or say that a current rate is required.
- Tell visitors what happens after they submit the intake form.
- Use clear availability and response expectations only when the client confirms them.

### Reduce effort and sacrifice

- Pre-fill the WhatsApp inquiry.
- Ask only for information needed to quote.
- Explain which planning work the operator handles.
- Keep package comparisons and options understandable.
- Avoid forcing visitors to download a PDF before understanding the offer.

## Three Tensions

### Survival tension

Name the practical cost without fearmongering:

- Another weekend disappears into work and errands.
- The group organizer is exposed if the supplier fails.
- A vague package can become expensive through hidden extras.
- Cheap travel can create stress through poor planning and unreliable execution.

### Identity tension

Mirror who the buyer believes they are or wants to be:

- A parent who gives children real Kenyan memories.
- A partner who planned something thoughtful.
- A professional who protects their time.
- An organizer who handled every detail properly.
- A Kenyan who still wants to discover the country well.

### Progress tension

Show the immediate future:

- The dates are chosen.
- The route and inclusions are clear.
- The quote arrives with options.
- The buyer can decide without chasing five vague posters.

## Page Copy Sequence

Use this as a default, then adapt it to the package:

1. **Hero outcome or pressure:** one strong headline, one supporting promise, duration, route, and price context.
2. **Fast facts:** duration, departure, travel style, accommodation basis, ideal audience, current price status.
3. **Why this trip:** translate itinerary into an emotional outcome.
4. **Day-by-day itinerary:** remove uncertainty.
5. **Trust and logistics:** vehicle, guide, accommodation, meals, pickup, operator relationship, and proof.
6. **Included and excluded:** visible before the quote request.
7. **Price assumptions:** season, residency, sharing basis, group size, and options.
8. **FAQ:** answer the dominant objections for the page's audience.
9. **WhatsApp intake:** explain the small next step and what happens afterward.

## Campaign Landing Page Sequence

Campaign pages may be shorter and more focused:

1. Hook from the ad.
2. Specific outcome and package.
3. Three to five proof points.
4. Condensed itinerary.
5. Price basis and critical exclusions.
6. Trust proof.
7. Intake form and WhatsApp handoff.

Do not change factual tour data to fit an ad. Change emphasis, sequence, imagery, and persuasive framing.

## Human Rewrite Pass

Remove:

- Repeated sentence rhythm.
- Artificially balanced lists.
- Generic phrases such as `unforgettable experience`, `breathtaking adventure`, and `best safari ever` unless made specific.
- `This is not just X, it is Y` constructions.
- Excessive exclamation marks.
- Corporate abstractions such as `seamless solutions`.
- Long explanations of the website or funnel itself.

Add:

- Real route and timing details.
- Specific accommodation and transport information.
- Natural Kenyan context without forced slang.
- Varied sentence length.
- One direct CTA.
- A reason to believe the promise.

## Maasai Mara Example

Weak:

> Embark on an unforgettable journey into the breathtaking Maasai Mara and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Stronger broad package copy:

> Three days in the Mara, planned from Nairobi and back. See the route, stay, meals, game drives, park-fee basis, and vehicle option before you request your quote.

Stronger pressure-led campaign copy:

> When did you last actually escape? Take three days out of the traffic, deadlines, and errands. Holiday Kenya Safaris will plan the Mara route, stay, meals, game drives, and return to Nairobi around your dates and group.

## CTA Language

Preferred:

- Request your WhatsApp quote.
- Check dates and current KSh rate.
- Plan this trip on WhatsApp.
- Get a family quote.
- Ask about private vehicle options.

Avoid:

- Submit.
- Buy now, when there is no checkout.
- Book instantly, when availability still requires confirmation.
- Contact us, when the actual next step can be named.

## Copy Quality Gate

Before publishing, verify:

- Does the headline identify a real outcome, pressure, or package?
- Is the reader clear about the duration and route?
- Are price assumptions visible?
- Is every trust claim sourced?
- Is the main objection answered before the CTA?
- Does the page sound like a competent person rather than generated brochure copy?
- Is there one obvious next step?
